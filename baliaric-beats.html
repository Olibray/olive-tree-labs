The Concept

A festival, built backwards.

Most festivals book a lineup, sell tickets, and hope the crowd shows up. Baliaric Beats flips it. The crowd commits first — then the bill gets built around the people actually coming. No promoter risk, no half-empty floors, no acts nobody asked for.

Pledge first

Reserve your place with a free, no-commitment pledge. Nothing is charged until the festival is confirmed.

Critical mass unlocks it

Once enough people are locked in, the festival is go. The threshold is the green light — not a promoter's gamble.

The artists' book opens

We open the booking book. You suggest trance & prog-house acts and vote for your favourites — demand decides who gets the call.

Then you pay £250

With the lineup locked and your pledge honoured, you pay £250 in full — the lowest price there will be. General release then opens to the public at a higher rate.

The Proposed Venue

Locca Sea House.
Where the cliffs meet the sea.

Perched on the sea cliffs of Tegal Wangi in Jimbaran, Locca Sea House is our proposed home for the inaugural Bali edition — a sanctuary of nature and sound with panoramic views over the ocean, the runway, and Mount Agung beyond. Its theatre-style terraces drop toward the water, turning golden hour into a main-stage trance moment.

Where The Money Goes

Built backwards — paid fairly.

Every £250 ticket feeds one transparent pot. The bigger the crowd, the bigger the names we can land and the more there is for the acts who build the night around them.

Headliners

Paid from the booking pool

The acts you vote up top are paid directly from the collective booking pool — the combined total of every pledge that converts to a ticket, after festival running costs, of course. The more of us in the room, the higher the calibre of headliner that pool can secure.

Fee source: the booking pool — ticket revenue net of running costs, allocated to the most-requested names.
Support acts

A budget that scales with the crowd

Support and undercard artists receive a budget set by attendance — the more people pledge and show up, the larger the support pot grows. A packed floor means more stages, more slots, and more paid sets.

Fee source: attendance-based budget — grows with the number of confirmed attendees.
